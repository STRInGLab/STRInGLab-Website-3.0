# Design Language and Art Direction

## The feeling

**Editorial systems intelligence.** The work should feel like an operating room for decisions: composed, exact and premium, but warm enough to invite a conversation. It is not cyberpunk, not blue enterprise SaaS, and not generic AI imagery.

### Personality sliders

| More | Less |
| --- | --- |
| Precise | Decorative |
| Editorial | Corporate |
| Systems-led | Gadget-led |
| Calm confidence | Hype |
| Warm material contrast | Cold glass effects |
| Evidence and outcomes | Feature clutter |

## Composition grammar

1. Start with one dominant idea: a route, a model, an outcome, a decision or a product proof.
2. Use deep ink as the primary field; use paper as a deliberate interruption or evidence surface.
3. Show real structure: nodes, paths, tables, OCR boxes, operational maps, charts or UI fragments.
4. Let acid green identify the live signal, chosen route, selected record or key metric.
5. Keep diagrams sparse, labeled and physically plausible. Make them useful before making them decorative.

## Image direction

### Preferred subjects

- Abstract-but-concrete systems: data flows, routing paths, decision trees, model monitoring, document extraction and operations maps.
- Cropped, high-quality product interfaces that reveal a credible workflow.
- Material details that support the brand: paper grain, ink, technical annotation and restrained scan-line texture.
- People only when they are genuinely part of the story: working sessions, operational environments, candid collaboration. Favour purposeful activity over posed portraits.

### Avoid

- Glowing brains, humanoid robots, floating holograms, neon blue/purple gradients and generic server rooms.
- Default stock-image handshakes, call-centre headsets or team members staring at laptops.
- Decorative node networks without labels, opaque glassmorphism, fake code or meaningless charts.
- More than one dominant green area in a composition.

## Motion (when needed)

Keep motion short and functional: 180-280 ms ease-out. Favour a route drawing on, a KPI resolving, a panel revealing or a label underlining. Avoid perpetual decorative loops, springy bounce and parallax that competes with reading.

## Copy voice in visual assets

Use short, declarative lines. Lead with a business consequence, then a technical proof.

- Good: `Inventory decisions with a single source of truth.`
- Good: `84% containment. Built for production.`
- Avoid: `Revolutionising the future of intelligent transformation.`

Sentence case for headlines; uppercase mono only for labels, categories, dates, IDs and metrics.
