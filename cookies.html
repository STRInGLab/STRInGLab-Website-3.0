This Cookie Policy explains how S.T.R.In.G Technology Solutions ("we", "us") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on stringlab.org ("the Site"). This policy should be read alongside our Privacy Policy.

What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help the site remember information about your visit, which makes your next visit easier and the site more useful to you. Cookies can be "session" (deleted when you close your browser) or "persistent" (remaining on your device for a set period).

Cookies we use

1. Strictly necessary cookies

These cookies are essential for the Site to function. They cannot be disabled. They include cookies that remember your cookie consent preferences and maintain session security. No personal data is shared with third parties via these cookies.

2. Functional cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality such as remembering your preferences (e.g., newsletter form state). They are set by us or by trusted third-party providers whose services we use on the Site. Disabling them may affect your experience.

3. Analytics cookies

We use privacy-respecting analytics to understand how visitors use the Site. We use a self-hosted analytics solution that does not share your data with any third-party advertising networks. Data is anonymised and aggregated — we cannot identify you from this data.

These cookies track: pages visited, time on page, referral source, approximate geographic region (country level), browser and device type. No cross-site tracking. No advertising profiles.

Cookies we do not use

We do not use:

  • Advertising or targeting cookies
  • Social media tracking pixels from third-party platforms
  • Cookies that track you across other websites
  • Any third-party analytics service that sells your data (e.g., Google Analytics is not used on this site)

Managing cookies

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings. Instructions for the most common browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

Note that disabling strictly necessary cookies may cause parts of the Site to not function correctly.
